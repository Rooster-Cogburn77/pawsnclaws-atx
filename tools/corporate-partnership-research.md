# Austin Tech Companies Corporate Partnership Research
## For PawsNClaws ATX Nonprofit Outreach

*Research compiled: January 2026*

---

## Executive Summary

This research identifies the top 10 Austin tech companies plus additional mid-size targets for corporate partnership outreach. For each company, we've documented CSR programs, employee giving platforms, matching gift details, and actionable next steps for nonprofit animal welfare partnership outreach.

---

## TIER 1: Major Austin Tech Companies

### 1. Apple (Austin Campus)

**Austin Presence:** Major campus in Austin, TX with significant operations including a 600,000-gallon rainwater cistern for sustainability

**CSR Program Name:** Apple Environmental and Social Responsibility / Apple Employee Giving

**Employee Giving Platform:**
- **Benevity** (accessed via AppleWeb search for "Employee Giving" or "Apple Benevity" tile in Okta)
- External portal: apple.benevity.org

**Matching Gift Program:**
- 100% to 200% match ratio
- Donations processed through GlobalGiving partnership
- Nonprofits must complete due diligence review with GlobalGiving

**Pet/Animal Welfare Initiatives:** Not specifically documented; program appears to support broad nonprofit categories

**Key Contacts:**
- Employee Giving Specialist positions exist (salary range $88,300-$133,600)
- Contact: apple@globalgiving.org for nonprofit eligibility

**Austin Community Involvement:**
- Campus uses drought-tolerant plants with rainwater cistern irrigation
- Part of broader environmental sustainability efforts

**Next Steps for Outreach:**
1. Register with GlobalGiving as eligible nonprofit
2. Complete Benevity Causes Portal registration
3. Target Apple Austin employees for individual giving campaigns

## Community Partnership Opportunities

These are not corporate sponsors but potential collaboration partners in the Austin animal welfare community.

### Campus Cat Coalition (UT Austin)

**What They Do:**
- Volunteer caretakers managing ~40 cats on UT Austin campus
- Reduced population from 100+ to ~40 through TNR since 2000s
- Waller Creek corridor serves as natural habitat
- Student organization "Meow Mates" (~20 students) helps in West Campus

**Partnership Potential:**
- Centralized donation processing for their operations
- Shared volunteer recruitment
- Food supply support
- Combined publicity/awareness campaigns
- They have established infrastructure we can learn from

**Contact Approach:**
- Reach out through UT student organizations
- Contact through Austin Animal Center (they coordinate)
- Daily Texan student newspaper connection

**Status:** On outreach list - not ready to contact yet

**Sources:**
- https://thedailytexan.com/2015/11/25/ut-policies-control-campus-cat-colonies/
